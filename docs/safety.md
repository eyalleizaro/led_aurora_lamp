# Safety Notes

This project uses bright LEDs, an external power supply, and a moving motor.

## LED Safety

- Do not look directly at bright LEDs at high brightness.
- Start testing with low brightness.
- Use a diffuser if the lamp is too bright.
- Do not leave the lamp running unattended during early testing.

## Electrical Safety

- Disconnect power before changing wires.
- Check polarity before powering the circuit.
- Do not reverse the 1000 uF capacitor.
- Avoid exposed wires.
- Make sure the power supply voltage matches the circuit.
- Make sure the power supply can handle the current needed by the LEDs and motor.
- Do not power all LEDs at full brightness during the first test.

## Motor Safety

- Keep fingers, loose wires, hair, and clothing away from the motor.
- Mount the motor securely before running it.
- Start at low speed.
- Turn the motor off immediately if it gets hot, makes unusual noise, or vibrates too much.

## General

- Test one subsystem at a time.
- Keep the circuit on a non-conductive surface while testing.
- Use a proper enclosure before regular use.
